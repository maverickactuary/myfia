Content comparison

UKAPW: better content needed
The following main pages in the UKAPW lack focus. The areas are not well explained.

  • Students
  • Members
  • Research
  • Communities
  • News
  • Events

We might expect so much more. For example:

  • Who conducts the research?
  • Who benefits from the research?
  • Are there volunteering opportunities?

No doubt these questions could be answered by sufficient surfing of the UKAPW - but is this an appropriate requirement? Can't the UKAPW simply be more member-focused and helpful?

SOA: a higher standard
(1) Availability: Search for the 2007 Enterprise Risk Management Symposium and soon find the 2007 ERM Symposium I note:

  • The ERM Symposium easy to find
  • The papers are publicly available
  • They can all be easily downloaded

In contrast the UK Profession seems very protective of papers presented at its Conventions. Why not be more helpful?

(2) Consistency: As noted in the main pane, the content is consistent with the navigation.

(3) More than links: While the UKAPW has lots of content in pdfs (sessional papers etc) the content written for web pages is much weaker. A good grasp of site structure would improve things but the UKAPW sitemap has gone from bad to worse.

Layout comparison

  • Column 1: UKAPW generally has navigation, SOA has core functionality. SOA is best.
  • Column 2: Main content for both sites. SOA is generally better, especially the landing pages and "deep content" e.g. conferences.
  • Column 3: A challenge for both sites, the main reason being that there are not always related links. Neither site has is ideal.
