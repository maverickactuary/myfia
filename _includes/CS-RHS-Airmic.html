Content comparison

UKAPW: better content needed
The following main pages in the UKAPW lack focus. The areas are not well explained.

  • Students
  • Members
  • Research
  • Communities
  • News
  • Events

We might expect so much more. For example:

  • Who conducts the research?
  • Who benefits from the research?
  • Are there volunteering opportunities?

No doubt these questions could be answered by sufficient surfing of the UKAPW - but is this an appropriate requirement? Can't the UKAPW simply be more member-focused and helpful?

Airmic: a fair attempt
"Top level" pages introduce their respective areas, while further relevant links are in the third column. Example introductions include.
  • About Airmic
  • Events
  • Sponsorship / Marketplace
Some instructions are rather "light" including:
  • Research (what type of research?)
  • Members
The members section could tell you:
  • What's for free
  • What costs money
  • What you have to register for
  • What you have to be a member for
  • How much membership costs
Lower level pages are typically well written:
  • Special interest groups
  • EU governance consultation
Pages are usually brief and to the point.

Layout comparison

The intention of the Airmic layout is clear:
  • Column 1: graphical links to key sections ...
  • ... followed by links to "next events
  • Column 2: main content
  • Column 3: navigation ...
  • ... followed by the latest research

The currently active page is helpfully highlighted in column 3's navigation.
