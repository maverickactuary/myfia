Content comparison

UKAPW: better content needed
The following main pages in the UKAPW lack focus. The areas are not well explained.

  • Students
  • Members
  • Research
  • Communities
  • News
  • Events

We might expect so much more. For example:

  • Who conducts the research?
  • Who benefits from the research?
  • Are there volunteering opportunities?

No doubt these questions could be answered by sufficient surfing of the UKAPW - but is this an appropriate requirement? Can't the UKAPW simply be more member-focused and helpful?

BOE: a higher standard
Every "top level" page has a good introduction to the area. Examples of this include.

  • Banknotes
  • The Bank's Financial Stability Role
  • Statistics

The same principle extends to lower level pages. Examples of this include.

  • BOE working papers
  • The Special Liquidity Scheme

Layout comparison

The BOE layout is consistent site-wide.
Column 1 is for navigation and column 2 is for main content. Column 3 is more interesting:

  • Related links (internal and external)
  • Website feedback (top level pages only).

The BOE site has a layout which appears, at first site, to be similar to the UKAPW. The difference is consistency of application.
