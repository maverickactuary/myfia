My attempts to report bad links

On 28 February 2011 I sent the following email to webservices@actuaries.org.uk:

The email:

Dear Sir / Madam
The link www.actuaries.org.uk/events/one-day/worshipful-company-actuaries-wca-annual-lecture-world-needs-actuaries seems to throw me into administration – it should presumably be Ronnie Bowie's recent "The world needs actuaries" speech, following up on his 2008 Faculty Presidential address.

The same problem occurs when you load www.actuaries.org.uk/news/articles/worshipful-company-actuaries-lecture-world-needs-actuaries and try to click through on the "more information" link.

I believe there are a large number of bad links on the website. Would you consider setting up a bad link reporting facility to make this process a little slicker?

One approach could be for each page to have a single "bad link report" button, clicking of which would send the URL to the webmaster as a potential bad link.

Best wishes
Andrew

The reply was less than helpful:

"Both the links below refer to the Worshipful Company of Actuaries annual lecture, given by Ronnie Bowie on 19 February, entitled 'The world needs actuaries'." I gave up.
